Spartan Showdown (iteration)

  • Instructions:
    • Defend both towers against enemies for as long as possible
    • Move player with arrow keys
    • Select traps by clicking UI button, then place by clicking screen
    • To place trap, must have enough money (prices above trap icons)
    • Eliminate enemies with collision, each player (not trap) elim. earns money
    • The game will gradually get harder (enemies spawn faster)
  • Changes:
      As this is an iteration of S2-D8 (link), there are various changes that have been made. One notable change is the addition of bombs as a new trap. Bombs are meant to be a powerful trap that will be very useful at higher difficulties. It is now the most expensive trap. The bomb can be bought and placed like usual, but, instead of being collision-based, it is time-based, where it will detonate after a short amount of time. It can eliminate an infinite number of enemies, but they must be within its radius at the time of detonation. I also added 'powerful enemies,' which act the same as other enemies but deal more damage. These enemies introduce a new level of prioritization for the player, while also adding some additional difficulty to balance the addition of bombs.

  • Originality: What is original about your game? I think that the game is fairly original. It features a pretty unique style, objective set, and set of mechanics. The theme/story has some unique depth to it also. Notably, it features a Spartan theme, a currency system to buy traps, different ways to eliminate and handle enemies, enemy decision-making, different types of traps, dynamic gameplay difficulty.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology? One interesting technical aspect is how enemies have some level of decision-making. Each enemy is initially focused on a certain tower, but if that tower gets destroyed, they re-route to focus on a different tower. Trigonemtry is also used in various ways. Notably, trigonometry is used to determine the path of enemies, where they must traverse towards a tower. Trigonemtry is also used in relation to vectors, where the x and y components of velocity are found from a velocity magnitude and direction (angle). The game also has an interesting difficulty-increase mechanic, where enemies gradually start to spawn faster (up to a max rate) as the game progresses. Also, a lot of updating, maintaining, and checking was necessary to ensure that all collisions work as expected. This relates to things like health, money, processing new traps and enemies, routing enemies, enemy-trap collisions, enemy-tower collisions, and player-enemy collisions. Due to the presence of money, a type of currency system exists to allow for purchasing of traps. The code is also written in a way that makes many settings easily-modifiable, which made balancing much more efficient. With the addition of bombs, some additional calculations became necessary. The bomb traps have a radius, so, on detonation, the distance between each enemy and the bomb must be calculated and compared to the bomb's radius. All enemies that are at a distance of less than or equal to the bomb's radius, on detonation, will be eliminated. Also, since the bombs have a short period before detonation, some timer must be tracked for each bomb.

You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype. The player can start/reset or stop the game. Once started, they can move the character (spartan) with the arrow keys. The player can eliminate enemies by colliding with them. Money can be earned by the player eliminating enemies with their sword (without use of traps). Money can be used to buy traps from the UI bar, which are selected, bought, and placed with mouse clicks.   • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game? The primary strategy will involve constantly and wisely placing traps, which will also require constant money gain. I tend to think that a layered approach will be most useful. This approach could look like a slime layer farthest out around mid-map, then a trap layer after (closer to towers), and the player will be around the traps, in a position that allows for money-gain and quick saves. After the spike-slime layer is created and the game difficulty is high, bombs will start becoming critical, in addition to maintaining the spike-slime layer. The player should also be conscious about what trap they have selected and be able to switch quickly. It could also be useful to prioritize one tower over the other, or at least realize when one tower is not worth saving. Similarly, it will be beneficial to prioritize eliminating 'powerful' enemies over normal ones.
